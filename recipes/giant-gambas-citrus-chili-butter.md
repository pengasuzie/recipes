## Ingredients

- 4 very large tiger prawns or gambas, head on, shell on
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Flaky salt
- 1 orange, peeled and segmented, to finish
- Lime wedges, to serve

### Citrus chilli butter

- 100g unsalted butter, cold, cubed
- Peel (no pith) of 1 lemon
- Peel (no pith) of 1 orange
- 2 fresh red chillies, roughly chopped (deseed if you want less heat)
- 1 heaped tsp smoked paprika (pimentón)
- A pinch of flaky salt

## Method

1. Butterfly the prawns: lay each one on its back and run a sharp knife down the length of the underside, cutting through the shell but stopping before you slice all the way through, so the prawn opens out flat. Devein and lightly score the flesh.
2. Make the chilli butter. Drop the lemon peel, orange peel, chillies, smoked paprika and salt into a food processor. Add the cubed butter and blitz to a coarse, vivid paste. Set aside.
3. Heat a wide, heavy pan with a generous slick of olive oil until shimmering. Lay the prawns in flesh-side down and press gently with a fish weight or spatula for 1–2 minutes, until the flesh just turns opaque and the shells go orange. Flip.
4. Add big spoonfuls of the chilli butter around the pan. As it melts and emulsifies with the prawn juices, baste the prawns continuously for another minute or two until just cooked through.
5. Squeeze in the juice of half the orange (use the squeezed-out segments to finish). Tilt the pan and spoon the sauce over the prawns once more.
6. Plate the prawns, pour the citrus chilli butter over the top, scatter with orange segments and serve with lime wedges. Eat with your fingers — bread on the side for the sauce.

## My notes

The chilli butter keeps in the fridge for a week, frozen for a couple of months — roll it in cling film into a log and slice as needed. Works just as well over grilled white fish or new potatoes.
