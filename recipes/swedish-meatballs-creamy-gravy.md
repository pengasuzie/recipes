Utterly delicious. Cosy cooking is in full swing with these seriously tempting Swedish meatballs. Makes approx 40 balls (I stuck the ones not on camera in the freezer for later). Serve with creamy mash, lingonberry sauce and seasonal green veg.

## Ingredients

### Meatballs

- 2 slices white bread (GF works), finely diced
- 1 large onion, grated
- ~70ml milk
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- 500g pork mince
- 500g beef mince
- 1 egg
- 1 tsp nutmeg
- 1 tsp allspice
- 1 tsp onion granules
- 1 tsp garlic granules
- Small bunch of thyme
- Salt and plenty of black pepper
- 1 tbsp oil, for frying

### Gravy

- 2 tbsp butter
- 1 shallot, finely diced
- 1 heaped tbsp plain flour (GF works)
- 400ml beef stock
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- Pinch of nutmeg
- Plenty of black pepper
- 150ml double cream
- Dill or parsley, to serve

## Method

1. Soak the diced bread with the grated onion and milk in a large bowl for a few minutes.
2. Add the garlic, Worcestershire, pork, beef, egg, nutmeg, allspice, onion and garlic granules, thyme leaves and a generous amount of salt and pepper. Mix well.
3. Roll into small balls (about 40).
4. Heat the oil in a wide pan over high heat. Brown the meatballs all over, working in batches so they catch colour rather than steam.
5. Transfer the browned meatballs to a tray and finish in the oven at 175°C while you make the gravy.
6. In the same pan over medium heat, melt the butter and cook the shallot for a few minutes until translucent.
7. Stir in the flour and cook for a couple of minutes until lightly toasted.
8. Slowly whisk in the beef stock, then add the Dijon, pepper, a pinch of nutmeg and the cream.
9. Return the meatballs to the pan and simmer for a few minutes until the sauce coats them.
10. Scatter with dill or parsley and serve with mash, lingonberry sauce and seasonal greens.

## My notes

From @feelingtastyy. Freeze the spare meatballs raw on a tray, then bag them once solid — easy weeknight dinner later.
