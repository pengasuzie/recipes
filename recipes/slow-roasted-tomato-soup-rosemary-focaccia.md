This is a really long recipe (all really easy to do though) so I'm not messing about with a huge paragraph about how fabulously warming and comforting and just outstandingly delicious this Roast Tomato Soup with Rosemary Focaccia is… so deep breath, here we go.

## Ingredients

### Focaccia poolie (day 1)

- 30g strong white bread flour
- 30g lukewarm water
- ¼ tsp fast-action dried yeast, bloomed in 1 tbsp warm water

### Focaccia dough (day 2)

- 500g strong white bread flour
- 450ml lukewarm water
- 1 tsp fast-action dried yeast
- 1 tsp salt, diluted in 1 tbsp water
- Plenty of olive oil
- Rosemary sprigs, to top

### Tomato soup

- 1 x 400g tin chopped tomatoes
- 1 onion, peeled and cut into wedges
- 1 medium potato, peeled and chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, skin on
- 1 vine of cherry tomatoes (~12 small)
- 3 regular tomatoes, chopped
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 2 sprigs rosemary
- ½ bunch basil
- Pinch chilli flakes
- 1 tsp tomato puree
- Dash of balsamic vinegar
- Extra virgin olive oil
- 750ml vegetable stock
- 150g feta
- Salt and pepper

## Method

### Day 1 — Poolie

1. Bloom the yeast in a tablespoon of warm water for a few minutes until bubbling.
2. Stir into the flour and water in a small jug or bowl. Cover and leave somewhere warm overnight.

### Day 2 — Dough

1. Combine the flour, water, yeast, a good glug of olive oil and the poolie in a large bowl. Hold back the salt for now.
2. Knead for 5 minutes by hand (or use a stand mixer with the dough hook).
3. Transfer to an oiled container with a lid. Rest 30 minutes.
4. Add the diluted salt and massage it through the dough. Rest 30 minutes.
5. Drizzle with olive oil and do a stretch-and-fold (lift one edge, fold into the centre, rotate, repeat all the way around). Rest 1 hour.
6. Repeat the stretch-and-fold three more times across the day, resting an hour between each.
7. Cover and refrigerate overnight.

### Day 3 — Bake

1. Take the dough out and let it come up to room temperature, about 1 hour.
2. Tip into a well-oiled roasting tin (roughly 30 x 20 x 6cm). Press your fingers all over to dimple it and let the air bubbles spread.
3. Rest 30 minutes while the oven heats to 220°C fan.
4. Press rosemary sprigs into the dough and drizzle with more olive oil.
5. Bake 35–45 minutes until puffed and deep golden.
6. Turn out onto a wire rack and rest at least 20 minutes before cutting.

### The soup

1. Heat the oven to 170°C fan.
2. Tip the tinned tomatoes into a heavy oven-proof pot. Add the onion wedges, potato, garlic cloves, chopped fresh tomatoes and tomato puree. Lay the vine tomatoes on top.
3. Season with salt, pepper and a pinch of chilli flakes, then scatter over the oregano, rosemary and most of the basil. Drizzle generously with olive oil and a dash of balsamic.
4. Roast 30–45 minutes until the potato is tender and the onion edges are browning.
5. Pull the vine off the tomatoes and discard. Pour over the stock, crumble in 100g of the feta and stir. Lid back on, return to the oven for 20 minutes.
6. Let it cool for a bit, then blend smooth with a stick blender.
7. Ladle into bowls, top with the remaining feta and the rest of the basil. Serve with torn focaccia.

## My notes

From [@dominthekitchen](https://www.dominthekitchen.com/2024/01/roast-tomato-soup-with-rosemary-focaccia.html). The focaccia is the project — the soup is easy. If you don't want a three-day bread, swap in a shop-bought focaccia and just do the soup; it still slaps.
