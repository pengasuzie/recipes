## Ingredients

- 750g–1kg piece of firm white fish (rockling, snapper or ling)
- 2 bunches bok choy, washed and left as whole leaves
- 2 cups snow peas, trimmed

### Asian sauce / marinade

- 3 tbsp tamari or soy sauce
- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 garlic clove, finely chopped
- 3cm piece of ginger, finely chopped
- 1½ tbsp honey

### To serve

- 1 red chilli, finely sliced
- 3 spring onions, finely sliced
- 1 cup coriander leaves
- Lime wedges
- Brown or white rice

## Method

1. Heat the oven to 200°C (fan).
2. Whisk the tamari, olive oil, garlic, ginger and honey together in a small bowl.
3. Sit the fish in a baking dish or lined tray. Pour over about two-thirds of the marinade and turn to coat.
4. Bake for 12–15 minutes, until the fish flakes easily with a fork (timing depends on thickness).
5. While the fish bakes, tuck the bok choy and snow peas around it for the last 5–6 minutes, or steam them separately if the tray is full. Spoon the remaining marinade over the greens.
6. Transfer fish and greens to a platter. Top with chilli, spring onion and coriander, and serve with lime wedges and rice.
