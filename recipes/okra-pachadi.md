Today's recipe features one of my favourite ingredients — okra. This South Indian dish is great as a side, either chilled or at room temperature. It always brings back childhood memories eating sadya meals on banana leaves. If you're nervous okra will go slimy, you can substitute it with cucumber, beetroot, pineapple, pumpkin or any vegetable you fancy. Such a versatile recipe.

## Ingredients

- 2 cups okra, washed and thoroughly dried
- 1 tbsp cumin seeds
- 1 green chilli
- 1/2 cup grated coconut
- 1 tbsp + 1 tsp coconut oil
- 1 + 1 tsp mustard seeds
- 10–12 curry leaves
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 1/3 tsp asafoetida (hing)
- 1 cup natural or Greek yoghurt
- 1 dried red chilli
- Salt, to taste

## Method

1. Toast the cumin in a dry pan until dark and aromatic. Coarsely grind with the green chilli and grated coconut in a pestle and mortar or spice grinder.
2. Heat a tablespoon of coconut oil in a pan and add 1 tsp mustard seeds. Once they crackle, add the okra with a pinch of salt and half the curry leaves. Stir-fry for 4–5 minutes.
3. Add the turmeric and asafoetida and cook for another minute. Stir in the coconut-cumin mix and cook for 2–3 minutes more.
4. Whisk the yoghurt with a cup of water and stir it through the okra off the heat. Check seasoning.
5. For the tempering, heat the remaining tsp of coconut oil in a small pan with the remaining mustard seeds, curry leaves and the dried red chilli. Once they sizzle, pour over the pachadi and serve.

## My notes

From @karancooks. Dry the okra properly before it hits the pan or it'll go slimy.
