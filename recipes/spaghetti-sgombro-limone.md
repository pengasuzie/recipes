Tirmagno's midnight spaghetti — pantry pasta built on tinned mackerel, garlic and lemon zest. Three real ingredients beyond the spaghetti, twenty minutes, and the kind of dish you can cobble together at 11 pm and still feel pleased with yourself.

## Ingredients

- 500 g spaghetti
- 350 g mackerel in oil (sgombro sott'olio), drained
- 3–4 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- Zest of 1 large lemon
- Extra-virgin olive oil
- Salt
- Black pepper

## Method

1. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to the boil and cook the spaghetti to al dente.
2. While the pasta cooks, warm a generous glug of extra-virgin olive oil in a wide pan over low-medium heat. Add the garlic and let it perfume the oil without colouring.
3. Flake the drained mackerel into the pan and break it up roughly with a wooden spoon. Warm through.
4. Reserve a mug of pasta water, then drag the spaghetti straight into the pan. Add a splash of pasta water and toss vigorously until the sauce clings.
5. Off the heat, grate over the lemon zest, crack in black pepper and finish with another drizzle of olive oil. Serve immediately.
