If you're craving a quick, delicious meal that's sweet, sour, salty and a little smokey, then this recipe is for you.

## Ingredients

- 250g dried rice noodles
- Thai chilli powder, to serve
- Lime wedges, to serve

### Pad Thai sauce

- 2 1/2 tbsp tamarind puree
- 3 1/2 tbsp brown sugar
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 2 tbsp oyster sauce

### Stir Fry

- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 3–4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 small onion, sliced
- 200g chicken thigh, thinly sliced
- 150g prawn tails, peeled, deveined
- 3 spring onions (or a handful of garlic chives), cut into 3cm pieces, white and green parts separated
- 2 eggs, lightly whisked
- 1 1/2 cups beansprouts, plus extra to serve
- 1/2 cup firm tofu, sliced into strips
- 1/3 cup finely chopped peanuts, plus extra to serve

## Method

1. Soak the noodles in boiling water for about 5 minutes, then drain and rinse under cold water to stop them cooking further. Set aside.
2. Mix all the Pad Thai sauce ingredients and set aside.
3. Heat the oil in a wok or large pan over high heat. Add the garlic and onion and stir-fry until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add the chicken and stir-fry until just cooked, then add the prawn tails and the white parts of the spring onion.
4. Push everything to the side of the wok and pour in the beaten eggs. Scramble with a spatula or wooden spoon, then mix everything together.
5. Add the beansprouts, tofu, cooked noodles and Pad Thai sauce. Gently toss until everything is well coated. Add the green parts of the spring onion and the peanuts, then gently toss for a further 1–2 minutes.
6. Transfer to a serving plate and top with fresh beansprouts, chilli powder, extra peanuts and a squeeze of fresh lime juice to finish.

## My notes
