Ixta Belfrage's brilliant Italo-Mexican mash-up: fresh lasagne sheets rolled around lime-spiked chicken and cumin béchamel, baked in a chipotle-spiked salsa roja, then finished with a bright salsa fresca. Comfort food with a kick.

## Ingredients

- 350g leftover roast chicken, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp lime juice
- ½ tsp fine salt
- 8 fresh lasagne sheets
- Lime wedges, to serve

### Cumin béchamel
- 40g unsalted butter
- 40g plain flour
- 450g whole milk
- 1 clove garlic, finely grated
- 1½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp chipotle flakes
- 100g mozzarella, drained and chopped
- 1 tsp fine salt

### Salsa roja
- 250g chicken stock (or water)
- 200g tomato passata
- 30g tomato paste
- 2 cloves garlic, finely grated
- 15g unsalted butter, melted
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp dried Mexican (or regular) oregano
- 1 tsp chipotle flakes
- ½ tsp fine salt

### Salsa fresca
- 1 medium ripe tomato, finely chopped (~100g)
- 1 red chilli, finely chopped
- ¼ onion, finely chopped (~30g)
- 1½ tbsp olive oil, plus more for serving
- 4 tsp lime juice
- ¼ tsp fine salt
- 2 tbsp finely chopped fresh coriander

## Method

1. Mix the chicken with the lime juice and salt and set aside.
2. For the béchamel, melt the butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in the flour to a smooth, thick paste and cook for 1 minute, whisking continuously. Slowly pour in the milk, whisking vigorously to avoid lumps. Cook for about 1½ minutes, still whisking, until thickened to a smooth béchamel.
3. Off the heat, stir in the garlic, cumin, chipotle, mozzarella and salt until the mozzarella melts. Transfer half the béchamel to a separate container and set aside. Stir the chicken into the béchamel remaining in the pan.
4. Whisk all the salsa roja ingredients together in a bowl. Pour two-thirds of the salsa into a 23 × 33cm baking dish.
5. Preheat the oven to 200°C (180°C fan).
6. Place the lasagne sheets in a large heatproof container, cover with boiling water and let soften for a few minutes.
7. One sheet at a time, lift the lasagne out of the water and lay on a work surface. If the sheets are around 15 × 20cm, have a shorter side facing you; if much smaller, have a longer side facing you. Arrange about 75g of the chicken mix along the side closest to you, roll up tightly and place in the dish seam-side down. Repeat until the dish is filled.
8. Pour over the remaining salsa roja, then spoon over the reserved béchamel. Drizzle with olive oil and bake for 30 minutes.
9. Meanwhile, mix the tomato, chilli, onion, oil, lime juice and salt for the salsa fresca.
10. Just before serving, stir the coriander through the salsa fresca. Top the hot cannelloni with the salsa fresca, drizzle with more olive oil and serve with lime wedges.

## My notes

For a vegetarian version, swap the chicken for marinated tofu. To go vegan, use dairy-free butter, milk and mozzarella, and dried lasagne sheets (boil first).
