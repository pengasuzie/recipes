Loaded juicy pieces of chicken, melt-in-your-mouth potatoes and cooked in one pan to maximise flavour and minimise clean up. Super flavourful, hearty, easy to make, budget-friendly, and can be adjusted to feed a crowd. The chicken and potatoes are Whole30 and can be enjoyed with a side salad or vegetables — but I highly recommend serving it with the quick-and-easy garlicky yoghurt sauce for an extra boost of flavour (substitute coconut yoghurt to keep it dairy-free).

## Ingredients

- 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, divided
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- 1 tsp dried oregano, divided
- ½ tsp dried thyme
- 60 ml lemon juice, divided
- 6 cloves garlic, finely chopped, divided
- Kosher salt and black pepper
- 500 ml chicken broth
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 700 g yellow potatoes (Yukon Gold), peeled and cut into 5 cm chunks
- 60 ml kalamata olives
- 1 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped, to garnish

### Garlicky yoghurt sauce
- 80 ml Greek yoghurt
- 1 clove garlic, finely grated
- 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 tsp lemon juice
- ¼ tsp dried oregano
- ¼ tsp kosher salt
- ⅛ tsp black pepper

## Method

1. Combine the chicken with 1 tbsp olive oil, the rosemary, ½ tsp oregano, thyme, 2 tbsp lemon juice, 3 cloves of the garlic, salt and pepper. Cover and refrigerate 1–6 hours.
2. Heat the oven to 220°C.
3. Heat the remaining 2 tbsp olive oil in a large oven-safe pan over medium heat. Add the chicken skin-side down and cook undisturbed for 6–8 minutes until deeply golden. Transfer to a plate.
4. Return the pan to medium heat, pour in the chicken broth and scrape up the brown bits. Stir in the potatoes, the remaining 3 cloves of garlic, the mustard, 2 tbsp lemon juice and ½ tsp oregano.
5. Transfer the pan to the oven and roast for about 40 minutes, until the potatoes are golden and just fork-tender.
6. Nestle the chicken back into the potatoes, scatter over the olives, and return to the oven for another ~12 minutes, until the chicken hits 74°C internally and the potatoes are fully tender.
7. While the chicken finishes, stir together everything for the yoghurt sauce and taste for seasoning.
8. Garnish with parsley and serve straight from the pan with the yoghurt sauce alongside.
