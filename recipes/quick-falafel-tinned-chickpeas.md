Jamie's shortcut falafel — tinned chickpeas blitzed with harissa, allspice and coriander, then pan-fried into crisp patties. Stuffed into warm pittas with whatever toppings are around. On the table in fifteen minutes.

## Ingredients

- 2 × 400g tins chickpeas, drained
- 1 lemon
- 1 heaped tbsp harissa
- 1 heaped tsp allspice
- 1 heaped tbsp plain flour
- 1 bunch fresh coriander (30g)
- Olive oil
- 4 pitta breads
- 2 sprigs fresh mint
- Sea salt and black pepper

### Coriander & lemon yoghurt

- Reserved coriander leaves (from above)
- ½ garlic clove, grated
- 6 tbsp natural yoghurt
- Juice of ½ lemon

### Other topping ideas

- Tahini sauce: 4 tbsp tahini loosened with boiling water and lemon juice, pinch of salt, optional chilli sauce
- Quick pickled red cabbage: shredded, scrunched with salt and 4 tbsp vinegar, rest 10+ min
- Quick pickled chillies: sliced, salt and 4 tbsp vinegar, rest 10+ min
- Cucumber & mint salad: scratched cucumber, chopped mint, red wine vinegar and EVOO

## Method

1. Drain the chickpeas and tip into a food processor. Finely grate in the lemon zest, then add a good pinch of salt and pepper, the harissa, allspice, flour and coriander stalks (reserve the leaves for the yoghurt). Blitz until smooth, scraping down once or twice.
2. Scrape the mixture out and, with clean wet hands, quickly divide and shape into 16 small patties.
3. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large non-stick frying pan over medium heat. Lay in the falafels and cook for about 6 minutes, turning halfway, until golden and crisp on both sides.
4. While they cook, mix the reserved coriander leaves with the grated garlic, yoghurt and juice of half a lemon. Season.
5. Warm the pittas in the oven, or toast directly over a gas flame using tongs.
6. Load the warm pittas with falafels, a spoon of the yoghurt and any toppings going. Pick over mint leaves and serve.

## My notes

The harissa does most of the heavy lifting — use a good one. Mixture is wet on purpose; don't be tempted to add more flour or they'll go cakey. Leftover patties reheat well in a hot dry pan the next day.
