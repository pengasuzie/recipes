Chef Luca Corleone's twist on vodka sauce — the base is blended with a roasted sweet pepper for sweetness and body, then finished with a heap of stracciatella instead of cream.

## Ingredients

- 200g paccheri
- 1 sweet red pepper
- ½ onion, diced
- 1 cup tomato passata
- A good splash of vodka (about 60ml)
- Stracciatella, a generous spoon (or a burrata, blended)
- A few basil leaves
- Grana Padano or Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated
- Extra virgin olive oil
- Black pepper
- Salt

## Method

1. Roast the whole pepper in a hot oven (220°C) until the skin is blackened and blistered, ~25 minutes. Drop it straight into a paper bag, fold the top shut and leave to steam for 10 minutes — the skin slips off easily. Discard skin, stem and seeds.
2. In a wide pan, sweat the diced onion in a glug of olive oil until translucent.
3. Tip the softened onion into a blender with the peeled pepper and the cup of passata. Blitz until smooth.
4. Get the paccheri into well-salted boiling water.
5. Wipe out the pan, set it back on the heat, add the vodka and (carefully) flambé it. Let the flame die down and the alcohol cook off.
6. Pour the blended sauce into the pan, season, and let it simmer while the pasta finishes.
7. Stir a spoonful of stracciatella through the sauce along with a few torn basil leaves.
8. Drain the paccheri al dente and add to the pan with a splash of pasta water. Toss with a handful of grated grana to emulsify.
9. Plate, top with more stracciatella, a grind of black pepper and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil.

## My notes

- No stracciatella? Buy a burrata and pulse it briefly in the blender — same result.
- The roasted pepper is the whole point of this version — don't skip it. You can roast it ahead and keep it peeled in the fridge for a few days.
- Flambéing isn't essential; just cook the vodka off over a medium-high flame for a couple of minutes.
