Greek baked lemon chicken with potatoes. Those lemony juices are best soaked up with bread also saturated with juices from a Greek salad. A great weekday meal the whole family will enjoy — only 20 minutes to prep, leaving your oven to do all the work. No Greek kitchen makes a roast without lemon; the secret is in the marinade. Kali orexi!

## Ingredients

- 1 whole chicken, cut into 6 or 8 pieces (or a mix of bone-in cuts)
- 5 potatoes, cut into chunks
- 3 sweet potatoes, cut into chunks
- 1 onion, sliced

### Marinade
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3–4 lemons, juiced
- ¾ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tbsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp vegetable stock powder or paste (optional)
- Salt and pepper to taste
- ¾ cup water

## Method

1. Preheat the oven to 180°C fan-forced.
2. Arrange the chicken, potatoes and sweet potatoes in a large roasting tray. Scatter the sliced onion over the top.
3. Whisk all the marinade ingredients together in a jar or bowl, then pour evenly over the chicken and vegetables.
4. Drizzle with a little extra olive oil and sprinkle with extra salt and oregano.
5. Cover with baking paper, then aluminium foil. Bake for around 1½ hours.
6. Uncover, baste everything with the pan juices, and return to the oven until the chicken skin and potato edges are golden.
