Sprouts blanched then deeply caramelised in a hot pan, plated on a bed of garlicky cucumber-and-dill Greek yoghurt and topped with toasted hazelnuts. Paul Delrez (@delrezpaulchef) for Oikos — a side that's a meal.

## Ingredients

- 500g Brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 30g unsalted butter
- Flaky salt and black pepper
- A handful of toasted hazelnuts, roughly crushed
- A handful of watercress or pea shoots, to finish

### Tzatziki-style yoghurt

- 400g thick Greek yoghurt (Oikos or similar)
- ½ cucumber, grated and squeezed of excess water
- 1 small garlic clove, grated
- A large handful of dill, finely chopped
- A small handful of flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- Juice of ½ lemon
- 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- Flaky salt and black pepper

## Method

1. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to the boil. Drop in the trimmed sprouts and blanch for 3 minutes, then drain and pat dry — they need to be properly dry before they hit the pan.
2. Make the yoghurt sauce. Tip the yoghurt into a wide bowl. Add the grated cucumber, garlic, dill, parsley, lemon juice and olive oil. Season generously with salt and pepper and stir until well combined. Taste and tweak.
3. Heat the olive oil and butter in a large heavy pan over medium-high heat. Lay the sprouts cut-side down in a single layer and leave them undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until deeply charred. Sprinkle with flaky salt, flip, and cook another 2–3 minutes until tender and caramelised all over.
4. Spread a thick layer of the yoghurt sauce across a serving plate. Pile the hot sprouts on top, scatter over the crushed hazelnuts and the watercress, and finish with a final drizzle of olive oil and a crack of pepper.

## My notes

Halve the sprouts properly — uniform pieces means uniform char. Toasting the hazelnuts yourself in a dry pan for a couple of minutes makes a real difference.
