A weeknight chickpea curry — bold, warming, and almost entirely from the pantry. Stores well in the fridge, so it doubles as next-day lunch.

## Ingredients

- 2 large tomatoes
- 2 tbsp ghee
- 1 red onion, diced
- 2 cardamom pods
- 1 bay leaf
- 4 large garlic cloves, minced
- 2" piece ginger, grated
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp red chilli powder
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp curry powder
- 1/2 tsp cumin
- 1/4 tsp turmeric
- Salt and pepper
- 1 can chickpeas, drained
- 1 cup water

### To finish

- Splash of cream (optional)
- Coriander leaves
- Lemon juice
- Sliced red onion

## Method

1. Boil a pot of water, drop in the whole tomatoes, cover and steam 10 minutes on medium-high. Lift out, slip off the skins, pull off the stems, and blend to a puree (you want 1.5–2 cups of liquid).
2. Heat the ghee in a pan over medium. Sauté the onion 2–3 minutes until softened.
3. Add the cardamom, bay leaf, garlic, ginger, and all the ground spices. Stir 30 seconds until fragrant.
4. Pour in the tomato puree, season with salt and pepper, stir well. Cover and simmer 10 minutes.
5. Add the chickpeas and water. Cover and simmer 20–30 minutes until the chickpeas are tender and the sauce has tightened.
6. Fish out the bay leaf and cardamom pods. Mash a few chickpeas against the side of the pan to thicken the curry.
7. Stir through cream if using. Top with coriander, a squeeze of lemon, and slices of raw red onion. Serve with basmati rice or flaky paratha.
