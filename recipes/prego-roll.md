Peri peri is actually from southern Africa — when the Portuguese in Mozambique mixed African bird's eye chillies with garlic, vinegar and paprika. That's why the prego roll is a staple in every pub.

## Ingredients

### Steak and marinade

- 4 sirloin steaks (around 200g each)
- 200ml beer (lager)
- 4 bird's eye chillies
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- Zest and juice of 1 lemon
- 1 tsp salt

### Peri peri sauce

- 2 red capsicums
- 2 long red chillies
- 2 bird's eye chillies (go steady — they're hot)
- 1 red onion, halved
- 4 garlic cloves
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp salt
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- Zest and juice of 1 lemon

### To assemble

- 4 Portuguese rolls (or soft white bread rolls)
- Crispy fried shallots

## Method

1. Lay a piece of parchment over each steak and bash flat with a rolling pin or mallet.
2. For the marinade, blend the beer, chillies, garlic, vinegar, tomato paste, oil, lemon zest, lemon juice and salt until smooth. Pour over the steaks and marinate overnight.
3. For the sauce, coat the capsicums, chillies and onion in oil and salt, then char on the braai (or under a hot grill) until blistered and smoky.
4. Add the charred veg to a blender with the garlic, salt, sugar, oil and tomato paste. Blend, then finish with the lemon zest and juice and check for salt.
5. Cook the marinated steaks on the braai over a high heat, basting with the peri peri sauce.
6. Rest briefly, then slice and brush with more sauce.
7. To plate, drizzle peri peri on the base of each roll, top with the sliced steak, more sauce, and a heap of crispy shallots. Close with the lid and press down.
