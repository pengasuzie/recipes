A Keralan coconut prawn curry from Karan Gokani — barely any work for a lot of flavour. Swap the prawns for pumpkin, chicken or fish and the method holds. Serve with rice, hoppers or string hoppers.

## Ingredients

- 250g prawns
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 1/4 tsp turmeric
- Juice of 1/2 lime
- 1 tbsp coconut oil
- 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds (optional)
- 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
- 8–10 curry leaves
- 2 tbsp shallots, sliced
- 1 inch ginger, minced
- 2 cloves garlic, sliced
- 250ml thick coconut milk
- 6–8 cherry tomatoes (optional)
- 2 green chillies
- Chilli oil, to garnish

## Method

1. Toss the prawns with salt, pepper, turmeric and lime juice and leave to marinate for 15 minutes.
2. Heat the coconut oil and add the fenugreek and mustard seeds. Once they crackle, add the curry leaves, shallots, ginger and garlic with a pinch of salt. Cook until soft.
3. Pour in the coconut milk, then add the prawns. Simmer gently for 10 minutes.
4. Drop in the green chillies and tomatoes and cook for another minute.
5. Garnish with chilli oil and serve with rice or hoppers.

## My notes

From @karancooks. Keep the simmer low once the coconut milk is in — splitting will kill the silkiness.
