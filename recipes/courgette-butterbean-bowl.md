The best way to cook courgettes imo, low and slow so they caramelise beautifully along with the whole garlic cloves to melt into the sauce.

## Ingredients

- 2 large courgettes, cut in half and then into wonky triangles
- 8–10 cloves garlic
- 5 tbsp good quality extra virgin olive oil
- Salt and pepper
- 2 x 400g tins of pre-cooked organic butterbeans + their stock (the water in the can)

### For the cashew cream

- 1 cup soaked cashews or sunflower seeds
- 3 tbsp nooch (nutritional yeast)
- Juice of one lemon
- 1/2 tsp salt
- Ground black pepper
- 2 garlic cloves
- 60–80 ml water for blending consistency

### To serve

- Crunchy nutty breadcrumbs (1 slice of bread and 1 handful of almonds whizzed up in a blender, fried in olive oil, salt and garlic powder until crispy — let rest on a plate lined with kitchen roll until ready to use)
- Fresh basil
- Lemon zest

## Method

1. Preheat oven to 160°C (fan setting).
2. Chop the courgettes and peel the garlic. Add them plus the olive oil, salt and pepper into an ovenproof dish for which you have a lid. Toss and assemble so the fleshy side is face down.
3. Slow cook for 35–40 min, then remove the lid and cook for 10–15 mins more until slightly browning on top.
4. Remove and squish down with a fork. You can drain some of the oil and reserve for garnish if you wish.
5. Blend the cashew cream ingredients together.
6. Add the butterbeans, their juice, and 2 tablespoons of the cashew cream to the courgettes and gently stir together. No need to heat.
7. Top with the nutty breadcrumb mix, basil and lemon zest and enjoy.
