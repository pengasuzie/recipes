A proper French braise — beef chuck slow-cooked in red wine with bacon, leeks and tomato paste until it's melt-in-your-mouth tender, then finished with buttery pearl onions and mushrooms cooked separately so they keep their bite.

## Ingredients

- 1.5–2 kg boneless beef chuck, cut into 2.5cm cubes
- Salt and pepper
- Neutral oil, for searing
- 3 strips bacon, diced
- 2 carrots, peeled and diced
- 2 leeks, white and pale green parts only, diced
- 1 onion, diced
- 8 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp dried thyme
- 1 bottle (750ml) burgundy or pinot noir
- Beef stock, as needed (1–2 cups)
- Minced parsley, to serve

### Pearl onions & mushrooms

- 30 pearl onions
- 1 punnet mushroom caps (button or cremini)
- 4 tbsp butter or ghee
- Salt

## Method

1. Preheat the oven to 165°C (325°F).
2. Pat the beef dry with paper towel, then season generously with salt and pepper. Heat oil in a large dutch oven over medium-high heat and sear the beef in batches — don't overcrowd or it'll steam instead of brown. Set the seared beef aside.
3. Add the bacon to the same pot and cook until crispy. Add the onion, carrots and leeks and sauté until translucent, salting as you go. Add the garlic and cook another 2 minutes.
4. Stir in the tomato paste and cook until it coats the vegetables in a deep rusty colour, around 4 minutes. Deglaze with red wine, scraping up all the brown bits stuck to the bottom of the pan.
5. Return the beef to the pot along with the bay leaves and thyme. Pour in enough remaining wine (and stock, if needed) to just submerge everything — traditionally it's all wine, but half wine / half stock gives a lighter result. Season to taste, bring to a very low simmer, then cover and braise in the oven for 2 hours. Check at 2 hours — if the beef is fork-tender, pull it out; if not, give it another 30 minutes.
6. Meanwhile, prep the pearl onions: drop them into boiling water, turn off the heat and let them sit for 10 minutes. Drain, slice off the root end, and the skins will slip off easily.
7. Melt the butter in a large pan over medium heat. Sauté the pearl onions first until they're halfway cooked, then add the mushrooms and cook everything through, salting as you go. They go in separately so they stay firm rather than turning mushy in the stew.
8. Once the beef is tender, stir the buttery onions and mushrooms into the pot. Taste and adjust seasoning. Scatter with minced parsley and serve with mashed potato, polenta or rice to soak up the sauce.
