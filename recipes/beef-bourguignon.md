There's a reason beef bourguignon is one of the most popular and famous French dishes. The meat is stewed for hours in red wine, mushrooms and onions, and results in a delicious and hearty meal.

## Ingredients

- 1.5–2 kg boneless beef chuck, cut into 2.5cm cubes
- Salt and pepper
- Oil
- 3 strips of bacon, diced
- 2 carrots, peeled and diced
- 2 leeks, white and pale green only, diced
- 1 onion, diced
- 8 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp dried thyme
- 1 bottle of burgundy or pinot noir
- Beef stock, as needed
- 30 pearl onions
- 1 box of mushroom caps
- 4 tbsp butter or ghee
- Minced parsley

## Method

1. Preheat the oven to 165°C (325°F). Cube and season the beef, pat dry. In a large dutch oven with oil over medium-high heat, sear the beef in batches. Remove and set aside.
2. Add the bacon and sauté until crispy. Add the onion, carrots and leeks; sauté until translucent. Add the garlic and sauté 2 minutes. Add the tomato paste; sauté until a deep rusty colour, about 4 minutes. Deglaze with red wine, scraping up the brown bits.
3. Return the meat to the pot. Add the bay leaves and thyme; submerge with wine and 1–2 cups of stock. Season with salt and pepper, bring to a very low simmer, cover, and braise in the oven for 2 hours. Check tenderness; cook a further 30 minutes if needed.
4. Meanwhile, prepare the pearl onions and mushrooms. Blanch the pearl onions in boiling water for 10 minutes, drain, cut off the root end, and peel. In a large pan over medium heat, sauté the pearl onions in butter until halfway cooked; add the mushrooms. Cook through, salting as you go. Add to the bourguignon.
5. Mix everything together on the stovetop over medium heat, top with minced parsley, and serve.
