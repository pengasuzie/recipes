Another favourite from 2023! This is a dish I can't wait to make again when the good tomatoes come back into season. Pan con Tomate with sardines and salsa verde. Recipe in my book with grilled fresh sardines, but you can use tinned as here, or mackerel fillets.

## Ingredients

- 2 thick slices of sourdough
- 1 garlic clove, halved
- 1 very ripe heritage tomato (or 2 smaller ones)
- 1 tin of good sardines in olive oil (or mackerel fillets)
- Flaky salt
- Extra virgin olive oil

### Salsa verde

- A large handful of flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 1 heaped tbsp capers, drained and chopped
- 2–3 cornichons (or 1 small gherkin), finely chopped
- 3–4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- A pinch of flaky salt

## Method

1. Toast the sourdough over a grill, in a hot dry pan, or under the broiler until well charred on both sides. Rub the cut side of the garlic clove over the warm bread.
2. Halve the tomato through its equator and grate the cut side on the coarse side of a box grater into a bowl — the skin will be left behind in your hand. Season the pulp with a pinch of salt and a glug of olive oil. Tip into a sieve to drain off the excess liquid if it's very wet.
3. For the salsa verde, combine the chopped parsley, capers and cornichons in a small bowl. Add the olive oil and a pinch of salt and stir until loose and spoonable.
4. Spoon a generous layer of grated tomato onto each piece of toast. Top with sardines from the tin, then spoon over plenty of salsa verde. Finish with a final drizzle of olive oil.

## My notes

The tomato needs to be properly ripe — out of season, this dish falls flat. Don't skip the draining step, otherwise the toast goes soggy in seconds.
