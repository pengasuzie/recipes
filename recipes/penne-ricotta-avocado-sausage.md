It's almost like having a cacio e pepe, but made with ricotta cheese — creamy, delicious, and you will not want to stop eating it.

## Ingredients

- 500g penne (or orecchiette, or shells)
- About 500g Italian ground sausage
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- A knob of truffle butter (or plain butter)
- 2 garlic cloves, peeled and smashed
- About 3 cups (750g) ricotta
- 1 ripe avocado
- About ½ cup reserved pasta water
- Freshly grated Parmigiano Reggiano, to finish
- Salt and freshly cracked black pepper

## Method

1. Get a big pot of well-salted pasta water on to boil.
2. In a large pan over medium heat, warm the olive oil with a knob of truffle butter (plain butter is fine if you haven't got any) and the smashed garlic. You want to give it a moment to sweat.
3. Add the ground sausage, break it up with a spoon, and let it brown.
4. Meanwhile, prep what we're going to use with the pasta: tip the ricotta into a bowl, add the avocado and a season of salt and pepper. If you have an immersion blender, use it — otherwise just whip it with a whisk until smooth and creamy. Now it's time for the magic.
5. Cook the penne in the boiling water until just under al dente — we're going to finish it in the pan.
6. Scoop the pasta into the sausage pan. Add about half a cup of the pasta water.
7. Spoon the ricotta-avocado compound in and toss everything together so the pasta is coated.
8. Plate up and grate over plenty of fresh Parmigiano Reggiano — if you don't have fresh, use the grated one. Crack of pepper on top.
