Crusty Mediterranean baguettes studded with olives and rosemary. Overnight cold ferment in the fridge does most of the work — under an hour of hands-on time.

## Ingredients

- 450g plain flour (11–13% protein is best)
- 50g whole wheat flour
- 375g water
- 30g olive oil
- 12g fresh yeast (or 4g active dry)
- 9g salt
- 1 tbsp rosemary, finely chopped
- 250g olives (or sun-dried tomatoes)

## Method

1. Mix all the dough ingredients (everything except the olives) until well combined. Cover and rest at room temperature for 15 minutes.
2. Do one set of stretch-and-folds to build strength, then rest another 15 minutes.
3. Mix in the olives, cover and place in the fridge to ferment overnight (or up to 36 hours).
4. Preheat a baking tray, pizza stone or steel to 250°C.
5. Divide the cold dough into 6 pieces. Gently shape each one and twist to create tension. Place on baking paper and rest for 15 minutes.
6. Transfer carefully to the preheated tray and bake for 15–20 minutes until deep golden brown. Steam in the first 8–10 minutes gives a glossier crust but isn't essential.
7. Eat warm — no need to cool.

## My notes

From @herbifoods. The long cold ferment is what gives these the flavour — don't try to rush it on the counter. Sun-dried tomatoes work just as well as olives.
